## Ingredients

**Dough**
- 530 ml warm water 
- 1 tbsp sugar sugar
- 1 tbsp active dry yeast 
- 2 tbsp olive oil 
- 1 tbsp kosher salt 
- 600g bread flour, plus more for working the dough 
- additional oil for greasing the dough
- semolina flour and flour for dusting (optimally whole wheat coarse ground)

**Sauce**
- 1 can of crushed san marizano tomatoes.
- 1 orange (can be substituted for 1 tbsp of sugar per orange)
- Chilly flakes to taste
- Italian herbs to taste (I use a dried herb mix)
- Salt to taste

**Toppings (these can be changed as desired)**
- Low moisture full fat mozzarella (about 6 cheese sticks per za)
- Pepperoni
- Parmesan
- Fresh Basil
- Finishing olive oil (i.e. not evoo)

## Directions

**Dough**
1. Start the dough by combining the water, sugar and yeast in a large bowl and let sit for a few minutes. If the yeast goes foamy, it's alive and you're good to proceed (if it doesn't, it's dead and you need new yeast). 
2. Add the olive oil and salt and 5 cups (600g) of bread flour. Mix until just combined, then start kneading. Add just enough additional flour to keep the dough workable (i.e. not too sticky) and kneed until you can stretch some of the dough into a thin sheet without it tearing. 
3. Divide the dough into four equal balls and put them in four containers (ideally glass) and lightly coat the balls and the interior of their containers with olive oil. 
4. Cover, and either rise at room temperature for two hours, or put them in the refrigerator and let them rise for 1-7 days. (I prefer the long, cold rise.)

**Sauce**
1. Add the crushed tomatoes to a bowl.
2. Zest in the skin of the orange and add the juice of the orange to the bowl as well.
3. Add the chilly flakes, italian herbs, and salt to taste.

**Baking**
1. Place a pizza stone in the middle rack of your oven and preheat it to its highest possible temperature for at least an hour.
2. Take the dough ball containers out of the fridge to let them warm to room temperature.
3. Prepare a pizza assembly station. I recommend the following setup in a chain:
	- A clean large surface with flour nearby for preparing the pizza dough.
	- A pizza peel that is both covered with flour and corn meal.
	- Bowls with sauces and toppings.
	- Oven.
	- Cooling rack/cutting board.
4. Take a dough ball out of a container onto a well floured countertop and push it into a rough disk shape using your fingers.
5. Using the gravity stretch or a rolling pin (or whatever technique you want really), stretch the dough into a pizza shape. Try to get as close to slight translucency as possible without causing a tear.
6. When happy with the shape, transfer the dough via a quick lift onto the peel. Confirm that it is able to slide easily. If it is not able to slide easily, use a bench scraper to add more corn meal or flour under the parts that are stuck. Make final shape adjustments.
7. Quickly add the toppings to the pizza as desired, the longer the pizza stays on the peel the higher the likelihood of sticking. Generally <1 minute is ok for a well floured peel.
8. Slide the pizza into the oven, going as fast as possible to keep heat well contained.
9. Bake the pizza for as long as possible without splitting the cheese (it's really a game of chicken that is specific to every over, and the duration will change as the oven/stone are not heated enough to keep up with making pizzas). Generally, this is < 8 minutes for a max heat home oven.
10. Remove the pizza from the oven. Add any finishing toppings (fresh basil, finishing oils, chili flakes, parmesan) and let it sit for 5 minutes on a cooling rack before slicing and serving.

## Notes

1. You will probably need to add a lot more flour. The quantity I give here is just a base line to get your started.
2. This dough is relatively high hydration, which means wet hands will stick less to it when you are mixing.
